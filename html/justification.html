Design Elements

The design of the website started quite earlier on. I soon came up with the idea that I wanted to make a website in relation to food. Throughout the course of quarantine, I got more into vegan food and the idea that you could also make culinary food made totally vegan. That is why I made a website for a restaurant that serves culinary vegan recipes. Next to that I wanted it to be easier for those who would be interesting in making vegan recipes at home to have access to. That is why I decided to ad a kind of blogpost for recipes special for the chefs to interact with the visitors of the website and restaurant. From this idea I came up with the name &Chef’s for the idea that everyone from the ease of their own kitchen can make delicious recipes together with a chef. For the color palette I looked for a scheme that represents herbs and spices from all over the world (See Picture above for color codes). For the font I wanted to combine a commonly used font on restaurant websites (Open Sans) with a font that can be looked upon as modern (Courier). The combination of the colors, pictures and font styles needs to create the idea of a modern culinary vegan restaurant. In the flow diagram there is an easy navigation to run throughout the website.

Digital Prototype + User Testing

Due to Covid regulations I could not do an extensive research on my prototype. For my testing I had my household existing out of three people be part of the testing. For the test of my prototype, I made a questionnaire together with a feedback capture grid. The feedback capture grid is a great tool to gather feedback from participants on a systematic manner. The grid is divided into four blocks. The first block is the “likes” in this block there are only the positive feedback from the participants. In the second block next to the “likes” there is the “criticism” in this block there will be negative feedback and criticism about the prototype. In the third block under “likes” there is the block for “questions”. In this block there will be question the participant asked during the testing of the prototype. In the last block next to “question” there will be the “idea” block. Here are ideas that the testing part influenced. Next there is an image of the feedback capture grid:

The procedure of the testing went as follows. The participant went through a prototype session on adobe XD, where I would ask them the following question out of the questionnaire made. They will answer of do whatever the question portrayed. The following questions are asked during testing:

Questions from my testing procedure

  • 1. What do you think this tool/ website is for?
  • 2. When would you use this?
  • 3. Is there anything this tool resembles? If yes, what?
  • 4. What, if anything, doesn’t make sense here?
  • 5. How often do you eat at a restaurant?
  • 6. Was there anything that surprised you? If yes, what?
  • 7. Was there anything you expected to find that was not there?
  • 8. What was difficult or strange about this task, if anything?
  • 9. What was easy about this task?
  • 10. How did you find navigating to the task page?
  • 11. What was missing, if anything?
  • 12. What was unnecessary if anything?
  • 13. What did you think of the layout of the content?
  • 14. If you had a magic wand, what would you change about this experience/task?
  • 15. Did it take you more or less time than you expected to complete this task? Would you normally spend this amount of time on doing this?
  • 16. How frequently, if at all, would you use this feature?
  • 17. Try to list the features our tool has.
  • 18. Do you feel this website is easy to use?
  • 19. What would you change in this website if anything?
  • 20. How would you improve this website?
